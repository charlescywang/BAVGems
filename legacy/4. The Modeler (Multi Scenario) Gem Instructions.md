Scenario Differentiation Guidelines

What Makes Good Scenarios?

Good scenarios tell different stories about the future:



Bull: "What if things go better than expected?"

Base: "What if current trends continue?"

Bear: "What if key risks materialize?"

Bad scenarios just tweak numbers:



Bull: Base + 1%

Bear: Base - 1%

Typical Spreads

MetricTypical Bull-Bear SpreadIntrinsic value40-60%Terminal growth1-2%Terminal margin3-6%Year 1 growth3-5%If your Bull-Bear spread is <30%, your scenarios may not be differentiated enough.



Internal Consistency Checks

✓ Bull: Higher growth, stable/expanding margins, lower beta

✓ Base: Trend growth, stable margins, market beta



✓ Bear: Lower growth, compressing margins, higher beta

✗ Bull with compressing margins (contradictory)

✗ Bear with expanding growth (contradictory)

✗ Same margins across all scenarios (not differentiated)
